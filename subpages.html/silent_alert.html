„Silent Alert" application concept

The "Sensorycznie i na serio" hackathon winner project in category of individuals participants who were joined together in a team in hackathon day. The „Silent Alert" application concept alerting deaf people to the dangers in the area and help them evacuate. The information is translated into sign language. The application also provides three different ways of getting help. The recipient can call the relevant service, call an interpreter, or try to get in touch with someone nearby by using the question templates.

The target

Within two days, develop an application concept that will help deaf people in emergencies, warn them of the danger and make it easier for them to get help.

The problem

Imagine Monday morning when you get on the bus every day. You have a 20-minute drive, so you take the book out. Suddenly the bus stops, there is no stopping around, all the people get off. You see the driver screaming something, but you can't hear him. You are looking for some visual information about what is going on, but you do not find it. The message "Attention, evacuation" comes out of the speakers. The driver tells you what to do next. Unfortunately, you will not hear these messages - you are deaf. None of the passengers know sign language. To communicate, you have to use a notepad on your phone. Scared passengers strangely look at you, they are afraid, and you are still uninformed about what is happening.

Alarm situations are not always suitable for deaf people. The lack of visual warnings makes a large number of deaf people afraid to learn about the disaster too late.

The datas

It is estimated that there are between 30 000 and 100 000 deaf people in Poland and about one million people with less hearing loss. These people are exposed to different types of dangers, as are hearing people.

The solution

The Silent Alert application

It supports deaf and hard of hearing people in crises. It allows you to track threats and difficulties in your immediate area, to call for help if necessary, and afford deaf people to communicate effectively with others.

The design process

Research & Strategy

The competition

There is no application on the market that alerts the deaf about the dangers in the area. The existing "Alarm112" application is used to forward emergency calls to the emergency number 112 without the need for voice communication. Our idea could be an extension of this application.

Persona

Based on the canvas and talks, we have created a Persona.

The strategy

We started the strategy of our application with a map of empathy to get to know the user more closely. We also filled in the value propositions that helped us to find solutions for users' needs. Thanks to the hackathon, we had the opportunity to conduct an in-depth interview with a representative of the deaf.

Workshops

The workshop consisted of 3 rounds. During the first two rounds, we had 8 minutes to sketch as many ideas as possible. Then we discussed them together and commented. In the third round, we merged and improved the app together.

The solution in details

Bilingualism

Selection of Polish and sign language in notifications and alerts. Tutorial in sign
language and Polish.

Personalize

Customize the notifications to your preferences. The ability to receive alerts based on location or city choice.

Three degrees of danger

The type of alarm and information about it is suited to the danger.

Assistance in any situation

You can ask the emergency services for help, connect to the dispatcher or use the ready-made help templates, which are read out by the teacher or displayed on the screen.

Next Steps & Key Learnings

Thanks to hackon, the problems of people with disabilities have become closer to me. Lectures and conversations made me aware of the obstacles and barriers that people with disabilities face on a daily basis, making it easier for me to pay attention to accessibility issues when designing.


The next step (thanks to the organization "Sensorycznie i na serio") will be a meeting with the Government Plenipotentiary for Disabled Persons. We intend to continue working on our application.
